Black Friday 2024: The Ultimate Tech Shopping Guide

By Sarah Johnson
• November 18, 2024 • 15 min read
Buying Guide Deals Shopping Tips

Black Friday 2024 is shaping up to be one of the biggest shopping events ever for tech enthusiasts. With retailers competing fiercely for your dollars, we've compiled the ultimate guide to help you navigate the sales and score the best deals on electronics.

When Do Black Friday Sales Actually Start?

Gone are the days when Black Friday meant waking up at 4 AM on the day after Thanksgiving. In 2024, expect to see:

  • Early November: "Early Black Friday" deals start appearing
  • Week of November 18: Major retailers launch preview sales
  • November 29 (Black Friday): Peak deals and doorbusters
  • December 2 (Cyber Monday): Online-exclusive deals continue

Best Product Categories to Target

Our analysis of pricing trends shows these categories typically see the deepest discounts:

1. TVs and Home Theater (40-60% off)

Black Friday remains the best time to buy a TV. Look for:

  • Previous year OLED models from LG and Sony
  • Large-screen 4K TVs (65" and above)
  • Soundbars and home theater systems
Pro Tip: Don't be fooled by "special buy" TVs made specifically for Black Friday. These often have fewer features and lower build quality.

2. Laptops and Tablets (25-40% off)

Great deals on both Windows and Apple devices:

  • MacBook Air M2 models expected to hit $899
  • Gaming laptops with RTX 4060/4070 graphics
  • iPads and Android tablets

3. Smart Home Devices (50-70% off)

The deepest discounts are typically on smart home gear:

  • Amazon Echo and Google Home devices
  • Smart plugs, bulbs, and switches
  • Video doorbells and security cameras

Price Tracking Strategy

To ensure you're getting a real deal:

  1. Start tracking now: Use tools like CamelCamelCamel for Amazon or Honey for general price tracking
  2. Set price alerts: Most retailers offer email alerts when items drop in price
  3. Compare across retailers: The same product can vary by $100+ between stores
  4. Check price history: Ensure the "sale" price is actually lower than recent prices

Retailer-Specific Strategies

Amazon

  • Prime members get early access to Lightning Deals
  • Watch for "Upcoming Deals" to plan purchases
  • Amazon devices see the steepest discounts

Best Buy

  • My Best Buy members get exclusive early access
  • Price match guarantee extends through the holidays
  • Strong deals on open-box items

Target

  • REDcard holders save an additional 5%
  • Often bundles gift cards with purchases
  • Strong toy and gaming deals

What to Skip This Black Friday

Not everything is a good deal. Avoid:

  • Brand new flagship phones: Minimal discounts, better deals come in spring
  • Current-gen gaming consoles: Stock issues persist, bundles often inflate prices
  • Cheap tablets under $100: Performance is usually terrible
  • Extended warranties: Pushed hard but rarely worth it

Shopping Checklist

Before Black Friday arrives:

  • ☐ Create accounts at major retailers
  • ☐ Save payment information for quick checkout
  • ☐ Install retailer apps for exclusive deals
  • ☐ Sign up for price tracking on wished items
  • ☐ Research regular prices on target products
  • ☐ Set a budget and stick to it

Final Tips for Success

  1. Buy early for popular items: The best deals on hot products sell out fast
  2. Use cashback services: Rakuten, TopCashback add 2-10% savings
  3. Check return policies: Many extend returns through January
  4. Consider refurbished: Often better than Black Friday pricing
  5. Don't panic buy: Most deals repeat on Cyber Monday
Remember: A deal is only good if you actually needed the item. Don't let FOMO drive unnecessary purchases!
